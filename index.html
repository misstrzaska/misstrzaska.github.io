The dataset used for the following plots is from: Kaggle

The Teams.csv file was used within the dataset (specifically the teams from 2005-2015), with an added column of location to indicate where the team is located.

        Baseball is a sport that has been around for years. It has been a sport loved by many, especially by Americans. 
        Within baseball, there are so many statistics that a lot don't realize. One important statistic is taking a look at
        a team's ERA, which stands for Earned Runs Average. This stat is so important that one of the criteria for winning the
        Cy Young Award for pitchers relies on having a low ERA.

How is ERA calculated?

        The formula for calculating ERA is pretty simple.
         (earned runs * total innings) / innings pitched
        First begin with multiplying the earned runs by the total number of innings. Then divide innings pitched.

What number is "good" for ERA?

        The lower the number, the better! Majority of ERA's for the MLB are between 2.00 and 5.00.
